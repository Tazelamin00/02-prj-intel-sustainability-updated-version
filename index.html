Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel is founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore.

They rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004 microprocessor.

It ignites the microprocessor revolution and propels the future of computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 processor launches.

It establishes the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor.

Its 32-bit architecture ushers in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel records its highest annual GHG emissions.

The company invests in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals.

The goals focus on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction across the industry.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero operations by 2040.

The commitment covers Scope 1 and 2 emissions across global operations and builds on years of initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity usage.

This helps lower carbon emissions and accelerates long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit.

Suppliers, officials, and industry leaders collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
